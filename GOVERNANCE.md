# Governance

## Purpose

This document defines how decisions are made and how maintainership works in the OSSAfrica Community repository.

## Roles

### Contributors

Contributors submit and improve community content through issues and pull requests.

### Maintainers

Maintainers review submissions, curate content quality, enforce policy, and manage releases/process improvements.

### Org Admins

Org admins provide final escalation support for governance, security, and moderation decisions.

## Decision Making

### Content Decisions

- Normal content updates are decided by maintainer review and approval.
- At least one maintainer approval is required for straightforward documentation updates.

### Policy Or Process Changes

- Changes to governance, contribution policy, or moderation process should be discussed publicly in an issue or discussion first.
- A minimum review window of 5 days is recommended before merge, unless urgent.

### Escalations

If maintainers disagree or a case is sensitive:

1. Document the concern in the related issue/PR (or private channel for sensitive cases).
2. Escalate to org admins for final decision.

## Maintainer Expectations

- Apply repository policies consistently and respectfully.
- Keep review feedback actionable and constructive.
- Prioritize contributor safety and Code of Conduct enforcement.
- Keep this repository organized and discoverable.

## Transparency

Major decisions should be documented in issues, pull requests, or governance updates.
